Panda Interesting Facts

The giant panda, also known as the panda bear or simply the panda, is a bear native to south central China.
It is characterised by large, black patches around its eyes, over the ears, and across its round body.

Camel Interesting Facts

A camel is an even-toed ungulate in the genus Camelus that bears distinctive fatty deposits known as "humps" on its back.
Camels have long been domesticated and, as livestock, they provide food and textiles.

Chameleon Interesting Facts

Chameleons or chamaeleons are a distinctive and highly specialized clade of Old World lizards with 202 species described as of June 2015.
These species come in a range of colors, and many species have the ability to change color.

Cicada Interesting Facts

The cicadas are a superfamily, the Cicadoidea, of insects in the order Hemiptera.
They are in the suborder Auchenorrhyncha, along with smaller jumping bugs such as leafhoppers and froghoppers.

Pigeon Interesting Facts

Columbidae (Scientific Name for Pigeons) is a bird family consisting of pigeons and doves. It is the only family in the order Columbiformes.
These are stout-bodied birds with short necks, and short slender bills that in some species feature fleshy ceres. They primarily feed on seeds, fruits, and plants.
